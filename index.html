Brief Bio: Dr. Sushant Kumar is a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. He previously served as a Senior Project Engineer at the Centre for Mechatronics, Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, from March 2024 to December 2024. Prior to that, he worked as a Research Associate III in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT BHU), Varanasi, India, from November 2022 to February 2024. He received his Ph.D. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Tezpur University, India, and an M.Tech. in Bioelectronics from Tezpur University, India. He completed his B.Tech. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from Netaji Subhash Engineering College, Kolkata, India. During his Ph.D., he was awarded the Visvesvaraya Ph.D. Fellowship by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology, Government of India.

His primary research interests include compressed sensing, embedded machine learning, and deep learning, particularly their applications in rehabilitation robotics, exoskeletons and biomedical signal and image analysis.

Research Area

Efficient ECG Compression and Reconstruction in WBANs via Deep Compressed Sensing

Wireless Body Area Networks (WBANs) are widely used in remote healthcare for continuous ECG monitoring. Multichannel ECG (MECG) data provide significant diagnostic information but increase the transmission load, making energy efficiency a critical challenge. Deep compressive sensing integrates deep learning with compressive sensing to optimize MECG data transmission. It enables efficient compression and accurate reconstruction of MECG signals with minimal diagnostic loss, thereby reducing power consumption.
Recent advancements enable simplified ECG acquisition without medical experts, making in-home or ambulatory monitoring more common. Using minimal lead sets to derive standard 12-lead ECGs reduces complexity and improves patient comfort.

Multimodal Learning for BCI-Driven Robotic Exoskeleton Control Using Biomedical Signals

Multimodal learning enhances brain-computer interface (BCI) precision for controlling a robotic exoskeleton. Deep learning algorithms fuse brainwave, muscular, and kinematic signals in real-time, enabling adaptive, context-aware commands that mimic natural movement. Lightweight neural networks optimize signal processing on embedded hardware, reducing latency for seamless human-machine interaction.

TinyML for real-time vital signs monitoring and adaptive responses on edge devices

Deep neural networks demand heavy computation, which can be challenging for small devices and cloud systems. TinyML offers a better solution by running innovative, lightweight machine learning models directly on microcontrollers, analyzing vital signs (e.g., heart rate, hydration, movement) in real-time on wearable devices. It handles data locally by eliminating the need for cloud processing, reducing delays and energy consumption.
